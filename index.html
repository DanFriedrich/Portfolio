About Me:

From an early age, I've always been around computing. Since my first computer, a windows 98 machine, I've been all about wanting to know how they work, disassembling and reassembling them and of course, gaming.

When I was 8 years old, my parents gave me a Playstation 2 Video game Console and another world was shown to me. My fascination for video games grew. I wanted to know how they were made, coded, how every ambient and character was modeled. That interest grew and by the time I was 15, I enrolled in a course for Video Game Design which lasted 2 years. There, in the Microcamp technical school, I learned the basics about all parts of making a video game - from story telling to 3D Modeling and of course, coding.

As I finished my video game design technical course as well as High School, I decided to enroll in the course of Digital Games the FAESA college offered. There I made new friends and was introduced to new aspects of not only game design, but also web design, data banks and applications. From June to October, 2019 I got my first job as an intern in a company that provided mobility as a service, similar to Uber. There I learned Front-end coding in Java, as well as design in XML and CSS using Android Studio and PHP back-end coding.

As I concluded college I imediately started another. This time, Systems Development and Analysis. I deepened my knowledge of software architecture and found another Internship.
